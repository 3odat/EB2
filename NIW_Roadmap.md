# NIW Roadmap

This roadmap is based on the client packet in this folder. It is designed to help you move through the NIW case in the order the firm expects, with the highest-priority tasks first.

## How To Use This File

- Start with **Phase 1** and do not skip ahead unless the law firm asks you to.
- After each upload or completed tab, send a short message through the client portal so they know to review it.
- Keep all files in PDF format whenever possible.
- For any document not in English, arrange a human translation. Do **not** sign any translation or name affidavit until the firm tells you to.

## Phase 1: Start Here

Your first goal is to give the firm the basic documents they need to understand your background and open the case properly.

### Do Now

- Upload your most recent CV to the `My Degree-Related Documents` section.
- Upload documents for your **highest degree only**:
  - diploma
  - official transcript or other official academic record
  - degree evaluation, if the degree was earned outside the United States
- Create or update your Google Scholar profile.
- Send the firm your Google Scholar profile URL through the portal.
- After uploading anything, leave a message telling them the upload is complete.

### Notes For Degree Documents

- Use official degree documents, not unofficial versions.
- If your school does not issue transcripts, use an acceptable substitute such as:
  - a registrar letter
  - a diploma supplement
  - another official academic record showing degree, field, dates of attendance, and conferral date
- If your degree is foreign, the degree evaluation should confirm U.S. equivalency. A general evaluation is usually enough.
- If degree documents are inconsistent, upload them and flag the issue to the firm.

## Phase 2: Complete The Core Portal Tabs

This is the most important writing phase. The firm uses these tabs to draft your proposed endeavor statement, petition letter, and support letters.

### Complete These Tabs

- `Proposed Employment/Proposed Endeavor`
- `Research Background Inquiry Form (RBIF)`
- `Publication Record`
- `Recommenders`, if your package includes support letters

### What To Focus On

#### Proposed Employment/Proposed Endeavor

- Describe your future work in the United States clearly.
- Identify 2-3 specific projects you are already doing or realistically plan to do.
- Explain why your **specific** work matters to the United States, not just why your field matters in general.
- Keep your proposed endeavor aligned with:
  - your degree
  - your field of expertise
  - your current role
  - your future employment plans

#### RBIF

- State your main field clearly and specifically.
- List up to 3 relevant CET categories or specializations if they fit your work.
- Explain why your work has substantial merit and national importance.
- Describe up to 3 key publications.
- Describe up to 3 strong examples of independent researchers citing or using your work.
- Add any documented funding, awards, peer review, editorial roles, open-source work, or other notable achievements.

#### Publication Record

- List the publications you want included in the case.
- You do not need to include every publication if some are weaker or unrelated.

#### Recommenders

- List more candidates than you think you will need.
- Stronger current preferences in the packet are:
  - U.S. governmental or quasi-governmental signers
  - supervisors, collaborators, or advisors who know your planned work
  - recommenders with firsthand familiarity with your achievements
- The packet recommends **no more than 4 support letters** in most cases.
- Do **not** contact signers yet unless the firm tells you to.

### Special Note If You Work In Industry

- Industry cases may receive extra scrutiny.
- Your materials should show impact beyond your employer and its customers.
- If your current job is not closely tied to your proposed endeavor, gather proof that you are still actively advancing that endeavor through research, collaborations, open-source work, talks, or related projects.

## Phase 3: Review The Drafted Proposed Endeavor Statement

Once your proposed endeavor tab is complete, the firm will draft your preliminary proposed endeavor statement.

### What To Do

- Wait for the draft. The packet says this usually comes within 1-3 business days.
- Review it carefully in Microsoft Word.
- Turn on `Track Changes`.
- Edit for accuracy, specificity, and consistency with your real plans.
- Upload the tracked-changes version to `Drafted Documents Revised by Customers`.

### Important

- At this stage, you do **not** sign the proposed endeavor statement.

## Phase 4: Review The Petition Letter And Support Letters

After the firm has your completed RBIF, publication record, and recommender information, they will draft the main petition letter and any support letters.

### What To Do

- Review every draft carefully in Word.
- Use `Track Changes` for all edits.
- Correct technical details, project descriptions, dates, metrics, and role descriptions.
- Upload the revised versions to `Drafted Documents Revised by Customers`.

### Timing

- The packet says drafted petition and support letters usually arrive within 15 business days after the needed information is complete.

## Phase 5: Complete The Questionnaire, ETA-9089, And USCIS Forms

This phase is about the official filing documents.

### Your Tasks

- Complete the `Questionnaire` tab.
- Complete the `ETA-9089` tab.
- Review the generated forms when the firm sends them:
  - Form I-140
  - Form G-28
  - ETA-9089
- Sign the required pages in black ink.
- Upload the signed pages for the firm to review.

### Payment Forms

The packet says that for filings received on or after **October 28, 2025**, USCIS accepts electronic payment only.

#### Preferred Option

- `Form G-1650` for ACH payment from a U.S. bank account

#### Alternative Option

- `Form G-1450` for a credit card issued by a U.S. bank

### Payment Notes

- The packet strongly prefers `G-1650`.
- Do not split the same USCIS fee across both forms.
- If you add premium processing, prepare a **separate** payment form for the premium fee, using the **same payment method** as the main filing.
- G-1650 and G-1450 should be printed and hand-signed in black ink. Do not use digital signatures.

### Fees Listed In The Packet

Use these as packet-based planning numbers and confirm with the firm before final submission.

- I-140 filing fee: `$715`
- Self-petitioned NIW asylum program fee: `$300`
- Total for standard self-petitioned NIW filing: `$1,015`
- Premium processing, if requested: `$2,965`

## Phase 6: Finalize And Collect Signed Support Letters

This phase only applies if your package includes support letters.

### What To Do

- Wait until the firm says the letters are finalized.
- Then send the finalized letters to the signers.
- Ask signers to use official letterhead if possible.
- Obtain a handwritten or verified digital signature. A typed name alone is not enough.
- Collect the latest CVs of the recommenders if possible.
- Upload the signed letters and recommender CVs to `Signed Letters and Recommenders' CVs`.

### Helpful Packet Guidance

- Governmental or quasi-governmental letters are especially valuable if available.
- Dependent recommenders are now preferred over independent recommenders for many NIW cases.
- The packet includes sample outreach emails in Appendix C.

## Phase 7: Finalize The Petition Letter And Proposed Endeavor Statement

Once the firm has revised the drafts and checked the support letters, they will finalize the petition letter and proposed endeavor statement.

### What To Do

- Review the final versions for accuracy.
- Sign the finalized proposed endeavor statement when the firm asks for it.

## Phase 8: Upload Exhibits And Supporting Evidence

Do this **only after** the petition letter is finalized or the firm specifically asks for exhibits.

### Upload These Items Based On The Petition Letter Exhibit Index And Appendix C

- signed support letters and the first 3 pages of each recommender CV
- your CV
- highest degree diploma
- official transcript or academic record
- degree evaluation, if applicable
- publications and patents
- Google Scholar citation report or other packet-approved citation evidence
- notable citation evidence for the specific citations discussed in the petition letter
- peer review evidence
- awards and honors
- selective memberships
- funding evidence
- conference presentation evidence
- media reports directly about you or your work, if truly relevant

### Evidence Formatting Rules To Follow

- Provide PDFs whenever possible.
- For publications, usually provide the first 3 pages and highlight your name in the author list.
- If journal title or publication date is not visible, include a webpage printout showing that information.
- For citations discussed in detail, include:
  - the first page
  - the page where your work is cited or discussed
  - the references page showing your work
  - highlights on the relevant text
- For peer review, use thank-you emails or completed-review screenshots.
- If a document is not in English, provide the original plus a proper human translation.

## Phase 9: Filing And Receipt Notice

After all drafts, forms, signatures, payments, and exhibits are complete, the firm will assemble and file the petition.

### What Happens Next

- The firm performs a final quality check.
- The petition is filed with USCIS.
- You should later receive a physical receipt notice.
- The packet says receipt notice delivery is usually within 7-30 business days after filing.

## Do Not Do These Things Early

- Do not upload exhibits before the petition letter is finalized unless the firm specifically asks.
- Do not sign the translation affidavit until the firm confirms it is needed.
- Do not sign the name affidavit until the firm confirms it is needed.
- Do not use Google Translate or other machine translation for filing evidence.
- Do not mail hard-copy evidence unless the firm specifically asks.
- Do not contact recommenders before the firm tells you to do so.
- Do not rely on unofficial transcripts if official alternatives can be obtained.
- Do not use a provisional diploma if a better official alternative is available.

## Suggested Personal Work Plan

If you want a practical order for your own time, use this:

## Final Reminder

The packet makes one point very clear: the strongest NIW cases are organized, specific, and internally consistent. Your degree, experience, proposed endeavor, employment, supporting evidence, and support letters should all point in the same direction.
